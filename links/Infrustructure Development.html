Infrustructure Development

1. Roads

We are being linked to Open Bay by a logging road and recently being mandated as the Trans Islands Highway linking the two provinces. The road provides for our local market produce to be transported to Open Bay Station to be sold at the market. It also provides for transport to gardening as we do most of our gardening activities on OBT’s harvesting areas. Until about last year some of our people manage to reach as far as Kokopo following the constructed Trans Islands High Way that runs through the Matanakunai Ward to West New Britain. This year people started flocking into Kerevat and back for just a day or two depending on the PMV schedules. The only problem that is causing hindrance is the Toriu river causing difficulty to cross over due to no construction of permanent bridge. In the past we have a limit using only OBT logging access roads to Wide Bay and Baia in West New Britain. The road is unsealed and is being gravelled with sedimentary rocks from major rivers within OBT plantation area, most of which are lose and is meant for logging purposes only. Due to the looseness of the gravel it is very risky since cars and trucks can easily slip off the road in the course of high speed so speed limits are being stressed every time.

2. Water and Sanitation

A. Water

In here the main source of water supply is running water from the nearby creeks. In villages like Aubu-alaba, Vao and Vunapeo, they use both creeks and wells but usually gets dried up during dry season. It becomes very difficult when it comes to dry season in terms of water supply for these villages and so they have to walk distances to get avail of a water source. Water tanks are only for those who can afford the cost including freight from Town to the village. Most people use drums and malfunctioned deep fridge to catch rain water for bathing, laundary, cooking and drinking. Others use main stream channels like Matalailai, Valeleoa and Malani Rivers.

B. Sanitation

Primitive method of disposing human waste is the most popular one here, second to that is pit toilets. For pit toilets, people do use that but it gives off very bad smell that is why it is always unattended to. Sometimes it is being built but not used and in other areas of the ward it gets waterlogged and the waste floats to the top especially during wet season. There are no proper waste disposal procedures especially on sanitation given to the people and so people ignore the proper methods of disposing human waste.

3.Transport

The most common type of transport used here are the Land and Sea which also applies to our forefathers. That is by canoe and by walking long distances, lately the introduction of vehicles by OBT to cater for its operational needs also provided for transport services to the people. It also gave light to the usage of vehicles as a mean of transport. Common type of transport used for distant locations is sea and especially by outboard motor. With the development of roads (New Britain Highway), vehicles became a mean of transport by land to distant locations such as Kokopo and Kimbe. Air transport was available in the past from 80’s-90’s and unfortunately closed down leaving us with only Land and Sea mediums of transport.

C. Classrooms

The ward has a level 4 primary school and is fully equipped with four double classrooms containing library and office space. All classrooms are permanent, two of which were built in the 70s and needs major renovations. Elementary School has only one classroom that caters for all students up to E2. The is a need for another one to cater for the increasing number of enrolment.
